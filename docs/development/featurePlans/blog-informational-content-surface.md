## Problem

The site earns roughly 99% of its organic traffic from brand terms ("conka", "conka flow"). Only 17 URLs have drawn any impression in three months. The five commercial money pages are transactional-intent and structurally cannot rank for research-intent queries such as "what are nootropics", "vitamins for brain fog", or "best supplements for menopause brain fog" (the single biggest gap in the keyword dataset). There is no surface on the site that targets non-brand informational demand.

Phases 1 to 5 of the SEO programme fixed the technical foundation (canonicals, per-page metadata, Product + FAQ JSON-LD, sitemap, robots, descriptive H1s). The foundation is sound but it has nothing to point at for informational search. A blog creates that surface.

## Who it serves

Cold, non-brand organic searchers and AI answer engines (AEO) at the top of the funnel. Readers are routed to the PDPs via in-article CTAs. This is the cheapest acquisition channel available and it is currently switched off. Secondary beneficiary: the brand's authority signals, which lift the whole domain.

## Business impact

Opens a net-new, compounding acquisition channel that reduces dependence on paid Meta traffic over time. Success is measured against the captured Search Console baseline: non-brand impressions and clicks, plus a growing count of indexed, ranking URLs. Secondary metric: blog to PDP CTA clicks (content to product conversion).

## Appetite

- **Phase 1 (infrastructure):** 3 to 4 days. One buildable unit. Slightly larger than the original MDX-in-repo estimate because the Notion integration adds an API data layer, block-to-HTML conversion, and image re-hosting.
- **Phase 2 (content):** ongoing marketing stream, gated on Humphrey's machine populating Notion and the owner publishing via the `Status` gate. Not a fixed budget.

## Design system

brand-base (new). Mirror the `/science` and `/case-studies` page shell: `brand-clinical` section wrapper, `<Navigation/>`, `<main>`, `<Footer/>`. Note the known `.brand-clinical` mobile hero gotcha (it zeros the hero top padding on mobile), so blog pages need an explicit `paddingTop` to clear the fixed nav.

## Approach

Build `/blog` in Next.js reading from **Notion as a headless CMS**. Humphrey's blog-generation machine already writes posts into a Notion database, so Notion is the source of truth. The site fetches that database **server-side** (via the Notion API), converts each Notion page to HTML, and renders the pages statically with ISR (Incremental Static Regeneration). Because rendering is server-side, Google and AI engines receive full static HTML; SEO and AEO are not compromised. The metadata, JSON-LD, and sitemap are all generated by us from the Notion data.

Publishing is gated on a **`Status` property** in the Notion database: only rows the owner marks `Published` are fetched and made live. That flip is the human review and claims-compliance gate. No git step, no drag-and-drop, no redeploy: ISR revalidation picks up newly-published posts automatically.

The product CTA auto-renders from a `Related products` property on each row, so the Notion page body stays clean editorial content with no need for embedded components. Every informational post is therefore also a conversion path.

`/blog` (singular) is used because `/blogs/*` (plural) is already consumed by a permanent 301 to `/why-conka` (the old Shopify blog, `next.config.ts`). That redirect is left untouched.

### Content model decision (why Notion-as-CMS)

The model changed during scoping. It was originally scoped as MDX-in-repo (author writes `.mdx` files, drag-and-drop into the repo). That was superseded once we learned Humphrey's machine outputs directly into Notion: reading Notion removes the manual file hand-off entirely and makes his existing output the live source.

| Option | Verdict | Reason |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **Notion as headless CMS** | **Chosen** | Humphrey's machine already writes here, so zero extra hand-off. Familiar editing UI, a built-in `Status` publish gate, and no code or PR to publish. Rendered server-side (ISR) so SEO is preserved. Cost: an API integration, block-to-HTML conversion, and image re-hosting (see Risks). |
| MDX-in-repo | Superseded | Strong SEO and git review, but reintroduces a manual drag-and-drop step per post now that the source is Notion, not hand-authored files. |
| Headless CMS (Sanity/Contentful) | Rejected | A second CMS when Humphrey already uses Notion. Extra vendor, cost, and a migration of his workflow for no gain. |
| Convex | Rejected | A database, not an authoring tool: no editor, no preview, no media handling. Would mean hand-building a mini-CMS. |

**SEO guardrail:** all Notion fetching happens in server components / at build, never from the browser. Client-side fetching would ship an empty shell to crawlers and defeat the entire purpose.

## The content contract (the Notion database schema)

This is the regimented structure. Humphrey's machine writes each post as one **row (page) in a single Notion database**. The row's **properties** carry the structured/SEO data; the Notion **page body** is the article. Our loader validates each row on fetch: a `Published` row missing a required property is skipped and logged, so a malformed row cannot break the site or ship half-formed.

### Required Notion database properties (columns)

| Notion property | Type | Notes |
|-----------------|------|-------|
| `Title` | Title | The headline. Becomes the H1 and the base of the SEO title tag. Aim for 50 to 60 characters, include the target keyword. |
| `Slug` | Text | Lowercase, hyphenated URL segment, e.g. `what-are-nootropics`. Must be unique. The post lives at `/blog/<slug>`. |
| `Description` | Text | Meta description, 150 to 160 characters, no em dashes. |
| `Target keyword` | Text | The single primary search term the post targets. |
| `Secondary keywords` | Multi-select or Text | 2 to 5 related terms. |
| `Date published` | Date | |
| `Date modified` | Date | Same as published unless updated. |
| `Author` | Text or Select | Defaults to "CONKA". |
| `Hero image` | Files & media | The lead image, ideally 1200x630. Re-hosted by us on fetch (Notion URLs expire, see Risks). |
| `Hero image alt` | Text | Plain-language description of the hero image. Required for accessibility and SEO. |
| `Category` | Select | Grouping label, e.g. "Nootropics 101", for future clustering. |
| `Tags` | Multi-select | 2 to 5 topic tags. |
| `Related products` | Multi-select | Any of `flow`, `clear`, `both`. Drives the auto-rendered product CTA. |
| `FAQ` | Text (optional) | Optional Q&A. If present, renders a visible FAQ section and emits FAQPage JSON-LD. Format to be finalised with Humphrey (a repeatable `Q:` / `A:` convention, or a linked FAQ sub-database). Omit if the post has none. |
| `Status` | Select | `Draft` / `Ready for review` / `Published`. **Only `Published` rows go live.** This is the publish gate. |

### Page body rules (for SEO and AEO)

The Notion page body is standard rich text. Rules for the machine:

- **Do not repeat the title as a heading in the body.** The `Title` property is the H1. The body starts at Heading 2.
- **Answer-first opening.** The first paragraph directly answers the target query in plain language. Answer engines extract this; burying the answer loses the citation.
- **Heading 2 for main sections, Heading 3 for sub-sections.** Clear hierarchy is what AEO parsing relies on. We auto-generate anchor links from headings.
- **A link to a relevant PDP is automatic** from the `Related products` property, so the body needs no CTA. Inline contextual links to a PDP in the prose are encouraged on top.
- **Length guidance:** roughly 800 to 1500 words for a cornerstone post.
- **No em dashes** (house copy rule). No unverified efficacy claims (see Risks).
- **Notion blocks we support:** headings, paragraphs, bold/italic, bullet and numbered lists, quotes, images, callouts, code. Anything exotic (databases-in-page, embeds, synced blocks) is ignored on conversion.

## Ingestion pipeline (how content goes live)

Fully Notion-driven. No repo step, no redeploy.

1. **Humphrey's machine writes a row** into the Notion database with `Status = Draft`.
2. **Owner reviews and approves.** When the post passes quality and claims review, the owner flips `Status` to `Published` in Notion. This is the human gate; nothing is live until this flip.
3. **The site fetches server-side.** `app/lib/blog.ts` queries the Notion database (filtered to `Status = Published`) via `@notionhq/client`, converts each page's blocks to HTML, and **re-hosts images** (downloads Notion's expiring image URLs and serves stable copies). Pages render with ISR.
4. **ISR revalidation** (a `revalidate` window plus an optional on-demand revalidate webhook) makes a newly-published post appear within the revalidation window, with no deploy.

**Preview:** `Draft` and `Ready for review` rows are excluded from the live site, listing, and sitemap. A preview route (or Vercel preview deployment) can optionally render non-published rows for internal review before the flip. Deferred unless wanted.

   - Complexity: Medium. Dependencies: tasks 1, 2.
   - Files: `app/blog/[slug]/page.tsx` (new), `app/components/blog/*` (new, incl. `ProductCTA`).

4. **[Frontend] Listing page `/blog`**

   - Complexity: Small. Dependencies: tasks 1, 2.

   - Complexity: Small. Dependencies: tasks 1, 3.
   - Files: `app/lib/jsonLd.tsx`, `app/blog/[slug]/page.tsx`.

6. **[SEO] Dynamic sitemap entries**
   - `app/sitemap.ts` is a static hand-maintained list today. Map `getAllPosts()` (published only) into entries and spread them in, plus the `/blog` index.
   - Complexity: Small. Dependencies: task 1.
   - Files: `app/sitemap.ts`.

7. **[Frontend] Nav + footer entry point**

   - Complexity: Small. Dependencies: all above.

## Rabbit holes

- **Perfecting Notion block conversion.** Support the common blocks (headings, text, lists, quotes, images, callouts, code). Do not chase every exotic Notion block type (synced blocks, embeds, nested databases) in Phase 1; ignore what we do not support and move on.
- **Over-designing the article template.** Table of contents, share buttons, author pages, related-post rails: all deferred (related posts land in Phase 3). A clean, fast, readable article beats a feature-rich one for SEO and appetite.
- **Writing content during the infra build.** One real Notion post to verify against. The stream is Phase 2 and gated on Humphrey's machine.
- **The `brand-clinical` mobile hero padding trap.** `.brand-clinical` zeros the hero top padding on mobile; blog pages need an explicit `paddingTop` or they collide with the fixed nav.

## No-gos

- **No comments.** Deferred indefinitely. Comments need a datastore and moderation, invite spam, add thin low-quality UGC that can hurt the SEO being built, and third-party widgets (Disqus) drag performance and privacy. Near-zero ranking benefit for a brand blog. Revisit only under a deliberate community strategy.
- **No second CMS.** Notion is the content store. Do not layer Sanity, Contentful, or a Convex content store on top of it.
- **No client-side Notion fetching.** All Notion reads happen server-side (build / ISR). A browser fetch would ship an empty shell to crawlers and defeat the SEO purpose.
- No reclaiming the `/blogs/*` URL space; the existing 301 to `/why-conka` stays untouched. Use `/blog`.
- No `robots.ts` change.
- No per-post generated OG images in Phase 1 (use the post hero image, or the sitewide brand image, matching how the PDPs handle OG today).
- **No auto-publishing.** Nothing goes live until the owner flips `Status` to `Published`. The machine writes drafts; it cannot publish itself.

## Risks

- **Content is the real dependency, not the code.** The infra is worth building only if the machine produces a steady stream of publishable, accurate posts. An empty or thin blog is a mild negative SEO signal. Phase 2 must actually happen.
- **Notion image URL expiry.** Notion's file and image URLs expire after roughly an hour. Serving them directly breaks every image about an hour after publish, silently. Re-hosting (task 2) is mandatory, not optional, and is the single most likely thing to be missed.
- **Claims compliance on health content.** Posts about nootropics, anxiety, and menopause will make efficacy-adjacent statements. Blog copy is higher-risk than product copy. The content should pass a legal review before publish; the `Status = Published` flip is where that check lives.
- **Notion as a runtime dependency.** The blog now depends on the Notion API: its availability, its rate limits (~3 requests/second), and schema drift if a property is renamed. Mitigations: ISR caching (the site does not hit Notion per request), validate-and-skip on malformed rows, and the documented property contract so renames are noticed.
- **Malformed rows.** The loader must skip and log an incomplete `Published` row rather than crash the article or the whole listing.
