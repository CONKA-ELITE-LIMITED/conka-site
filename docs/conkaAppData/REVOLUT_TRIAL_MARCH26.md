## Featured Participants

### Ben Cox — Most Engaged, Strongest Improvement

**64 tests | Full data tier | Age 27 | 95th percentile**

Ben was the trial's standout performer in every dimension. He completed 64 tests across 18 days — exceptional engagement that created an unusually clear picture of cognitive development.

| Period | Avg Score | Tests | CV | Avg RT |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 84.5 | 11 | 10.8% | 418 ms |
| Week 1 | 88.9 | 36 | 4.0% | 284 ms |
| Week 2 | 92.9 | 17 | 3.2% | 291 ms |

**Score trajectory:** +8.4 points (+9.9%) from Baseline to Week 2

**Consistency transformation:** Score variation collapsed from 10.8% (Baseline) to 3.2% (Week 2) — a 70% reduction in score volatility. His results stopped fluctuating and started clustering tightly in the 90-96 range.

**Response time:** Average RT dropped from 492ms (first 3 tests) to 303ms (last 3 tests) — 38% faster without losing accuracy. His best tests in Week 2 regularly fell below 280ms.

**Archetype shift:**
- Baseline dominant: Sharp Reactor
- Week 2 dominant: High Performer (speed >= 3, focus >= 4, control >= 5, accuracy >= 90%)

**Sub-metric evolution:**

| Metric | Baseline Avg | Week 2 Avg |
|---|---|---|
| Focus | 3.3 | 4.0 |
| Speed | 3.5 | 3.8 |
| Control | 4.4 | 4.7 |
| Accuracy | 90.0% | 92.7% |

**CONKA insight:** Tests taken with CONKA averaged 90.6 — 4.3 points higher than tests taken without CONKA (86.3 avg). This held across 43 CONKA sessions vs 21 non-CONKA sessions.

**Wellness context:** Best results came when reporting "sharp and focused" and "peak" readiness (Week 2, March 20). Lowest test (61, March 7) was taken very early in the morning after reporting "little foggy" — part of a rapid same-session self-correction cluster where he followed immediately with 85 and 90.

**Narrative:** Ben's data tells a story of compound improvement. Week 1 delivered a jump in average score and a 63% reduction in response time. Week 2 then crystallised that into genuine High Performer territory. His score on March 20 — 100 — represents a perfect test, the highest possible score. It stands alongside a string of 94s and 96s as evidence of a cognitive system operating at its ceiling.

## Key Narratives for the Website

These are the headline story beats distilled from the data:

**Group story:** Six out of eight Revolut participants improved their cognitive scores over the trial. The group completed 129 tests across 18 days, 75% showed measurable progress, and the most engaged participant scored a perfect 100.

**Speed signal:** Every participant with enough data showed faster response times by the end of the trial — on average 20-25% faster. The brain was not just scoring higher; it was processing faster.

**Archetype unlock:** High Performer — the most demanding cognitive archetype, requiring simultaneous excellence across speed, focus, control, and accuracy — appeared in 0% of Baseline tests. By Week 1, it accounted for 18% of all tests in the group.

**CONKA signal (Ben):** Tests taken with CONKA averaged 4.3 points higher than tests taken without. His highest-scoring period was Week 2, when CONKA uptake was most consistent.

**Consistency (Ben):** Score variation dropped 70% — from 10.8% CV to 3.2%. Elite athletes call this "the zone." Ben found it after 11 days of daily testing.

**Recovery (Doris):** Week 1 was her worst period (mentally exhausted, high stress). Week 2 saw a full rebound and her personal best of 92 — showing cognitive resilience when conditions improved.

**Ceiling glimpse (Charlie):** Two tests, four days apart: 74 then 83. High Performer archetype on test 2. The data teases a ceiling that hasn't been fully explored.
